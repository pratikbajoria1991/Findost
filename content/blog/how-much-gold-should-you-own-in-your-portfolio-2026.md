Sensex fell nearly **700 points** this week on weak bank earnings and global jitters, and gold keeps hitting fresh record highs. So it's no surprise "should I buy more gold" is one of the most-asked questions in Indian investing circles right now.

Here's the honest, plain-language answer.

## Gold's job is insurance, not growth

Over the long run, equity has consistently outgrown gold. Gold's real value in a portfolio is different: it tends to hold up (or rise) exactly when stocks wobble, because investors around the world treat it as a safe haven during uncertainty.

That's why most financial planners suggest a **5-15% allocation to gold**, with **10%** being a common middle ground for a balanced portfolio. Think of it as a shock absorber, not the engine.

A few things this is *not*:
- It's not a signal to go "all in" because prices are rising.
- It's not a substitute for your equity SIPs and long-term goals.
- It's not something to time — nobody can reliably call gold's next move, including us.

## The four ways to hold gold, and how each is taxed

**1. Physical gold (jewellery, coins, bars)**
Making charges, storage risk, and purity concerns eat into returns. Held over **24 months**, gains are taxed as long-term capital gains at **12.5%**; shorter holdings are taxed at your income slab rate.

**2. Digital gold**
Convenient for small amounts, but it isn't regulated by SEBI or RBI the way mutual funds and ETFs are — worth knowing before you put in large sums.

**3. Gold ETFs / gold mutual funds (Fund of Funds)**
The easiest way to invest via SIP alongside your equity funds. Held over **12 months**, gains are taxed at **12.5%** LTCG. One catch: unlike equity funds, gold funds don't get the **₹1.25 lakh** tax-free LTCG threshold — every rupee of gain over 12 months is taxed at 12.5%.

**4. Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs)**
Here's the part that's changed and genuinely worth knowing: **the government stopped issuing new SGB tranches back in February 2024**, so today you can only buy them second-hand, on the stock exchange. Under Budget 2026 rules effective **1 April 2026**, the capital-gains tax exemption at maturity now applies *only* to the original subscriber who held the bond from issue to maturity. If you buy an SGB in the secondary market today and hold it, redemption gains will be taxed at **12.5%** (over 12 months) — the "tax-free gold" advantage SGBs were famous for no longer applies to secondary-market buyers. The 2.5% annual interest is still taxed at your slab rate either way.

## A simple way to think about it

- **Already have gold jewellery worth more than 10-15% of your net worth?** You're probably over-allocated — no need to add more.
- **Starting from zero?** A small monthly SIP into a gold mutual fund is the simplest, most liquid, most tax-transparent route today.
- **Chasing the rally because prices are at record highs?** That's the one reason *not* to buy — allocate to a target percentage and rebalance once a year, not based on headlines.

Gold, like every asset class, moves in cycles. Its job in your plan is to reduce how much a stock market fall like this week's rattles you — not to be your biggest bet.
